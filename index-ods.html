Overview

Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.

Targets

  • Reduce the global maternal mortality ratio.
  • End preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5 years of age.
  • End the epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, and neglected tropical diseases.
  • Reduce by one third premature mortality from non-communicable diseases.
  • Strengthen the prevention and treatment of substance abuse.
  • Halve the number of global deaths and injuries from road traffic accidents.
  • Ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health-care services.
  • Achieve universal health coverage.
  • Reduce the number of deaths and illnesses from hazardous chemicals and air, water, and soil pollution and contamination.

Progress

Information about the progress made towards achieving ODS3.

  • Significant reduction in maternal mortality rates in many countries.
  • Increased access to essential health services.
  • Improved vaccination coverage for preventable diseases.
  • Reduction in the prevalence of HIV/AIDS.

Challenges

Despite progress, there are still significant challenges to achieving ODS3:

  • High rates of maternal and child mortality in some regions.
  • Limited access to essential health services in low-income countries.
  • Ongoing epidemics of infectious diseases such as HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, and malaria.
  • Rising prevalence of non-communicable diseases.
  • Insufficient funding for health systems.

Solutions

Key solutions to address these challenges include:

  • Increasing investment in health systems and infrastructure.
  • Expanding access to essential health services and medicines.
  • Strengthening health education and awareness programs.
  • Improving data collection and monitoring of health outcomes.
  • Promoting international cooperation and partnerships.
